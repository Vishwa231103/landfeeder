    Tomato plants wilting despite regular watering

    Posted by: Rajesh Kumar • Location: Punjab • Date: 3 days ago

    Problem Description: My tomato plants are wilting even though I water them daily. The leaves are turning yellow from the bottom up, and some plants have completely collapsed. I've tried increasing water but it doesn't help. The soil is well-drained loam.

    Solution from Agricultural Officer: This sounds like Fusarium wilt, a common soil-borne fungal disease in tomatoes. The fungus blocks the vascular system, preventing water uptake despite moist soil.

    Recommended Solution:

    1. Remove and destroy affected plants immediately
    2. Rotate crops - don't plant tomatoes in same soil for 4-5 years
    3. Plant resistant varieties (look for "F" on seed packets)
    4. Solarize soil before next planting (cover moist soil with clear plastic for 4-6 weeks in hot season)

    Posted by: Dr. Verma (Agricultural Officer) • 2 days ago

    Alternative Approach from Experienced Farmer: I've faced this before and found that adding Trichoderma viride to the soil helps suppress the fungus. Also, make sure you're not over-watering as this makes it worse.

    Yellow spots on wheat leaves - identification and treatment

    Posted by: Priya Patel • Location: Madhya Pradesh • Date: 1 week ago

    Problem Description: Noticing small yellow spots on wheat leaves that gradually turn brown. The spots are oval-shaped and appear mainly on older leaves first. The variety is HD-2967, planted 45 days ago.

    Solution from Plant Pathologist: This appears to be yellow rust (Puccinia striiformis), a common wheat disease in your region.

    Recommended Solution:

    • Apply fungicide containing propiconazole or tebuconazole
    • Spray in early morning when humidity is high
    • For future plantings, consider resistant varieties like HD-3086
    • Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization

    Low milk yield in my dairy cows - feeding suggestions?

    Posted by: Amit Singh • Location: Haryana • Date: 2 weeks ago

    Problem Description: My Sahiwal cows are producing only 8-9 liters per day when they should be giving 12-15 liters. Current feed includes green fodder (maize/bajra), wheat straw, and 2kg dairy meal per day. Water is available ad libitum.

    Solution from Veterinary Nutritionist: The diet appears deficient in protein and energy for high milk production.

    Recommended Solution:

    1. Include leguminous fodder like berseem or lucerne (at least 1/3 of green fodder)
    2. Add 1kg of oilcake (groundnut or mustard) per day
    3. Ensure mineral mixture is available (50g per day)
    4. Consider adding 200g bypass protein supplement
    5. Divide concentrate into 3 feedings instead of 2

    Posted by: Dr. Yadav • 10 days ago

    Additional Tip from Dairy Farmer: Make sure your cows are getting enough rest and aren't stressed. We saw a 1.5 liter increase per cow just by improving shed comfort - proper bedding, shade, and reducing flies.

    Organic pest control for brinjal crops

    Posted by: Sunita Devi • Location: Bihar • Date: 3 weeks ago

    Problem Description: My brinjal plants are being attacked by shoot and fruit borer. I want organic solutions as I'm converting to organic farming. Currently seeing bore holes in fruits and wilted shoots.

    Solution from Organic Farming Expert: Here's an effective organic management plan:

    Recommended Solution:

    • Install pheromone traps @5/acre to monitor and mass trap adults
    • Release Trichogramma wasps @50,000/acre weekly for 4 weeks
    • Spray neem oil (5ml/liter) with soap solution weekly
    • Remove and destroy all infected fruits and shoots
    • Intercrop with coriander or marigold as repellent

    Posted by: Organic Farming Center • 2 weeks ago

    Traditional Method from Elder Farmer: We've used a spray made from crushed garlic (100g), chili (50g), and neem leaves (200g) soaked in 1 liter water for 24 hours, then diluted to 10 liters. Spray every 10 days. Also, ash dusting on plants helps.
